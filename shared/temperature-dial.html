Which setting when?

Low (0–0.3)
Facts, data, code, policy answers, customer support. Consistent and on-the-rails.
Medium (0.4–0.8)
Everyday writing, emails, summaries. A natural balance of reliable and varied.
High (0.9+)
Brainstorming, names, taglines, story ideas. Creative — but likelier to wander or make things up.

Four things to take away

It picks words by probability

For every next word the AI has a ranked list of options. Temperature decides how often it strays from the top pick.

Low = consistent, high = varied

Near zero it almost always chooses the favourite, so you get the same answer. Higher up it gambles on long shots.

Creativity has a cost

High temperature is great for ideas, but the same randomness makes it more likely to drift off-topic or invent facts.

It's a tool, not a quality knob

High isn't "smarter" and low isn't "dumber" — match the dial to the job. Many chat apps hide it; some let you set it.

Mini glossary

Temperature
A setting (often 0–2) for how much randomness the AI uses when choosing each next word.
Sampling
Picking the next word from the ranked options rather than always taking the top one.
Deterministic
Same input, same output every time — what you get at temperature near zero.
Probability distribution
The full set of "how likely" scores across all possible next words.
Top-p / Top-k
Related dials that limit the AI to only the most likely words before it samples.
Hallucination
A confident but wrong answer — more common at high temperature.
