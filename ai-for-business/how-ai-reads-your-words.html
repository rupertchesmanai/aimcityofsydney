What's happening

Four things to take away

Tokens aren't words

AI breaks text into tokens — whole words, word pieces, punctuation, even spaces. "Tokenising" is its name for chopping up text. This is why usage and pricing are measured in tokens, not words.

Words become numbers

Each token is swapped for a long list of numbers (an "embedding"). The model never sees letters — it only ever does maths on numbers that capture meaning.

Context comes from attention

Inside the network, every token "looks at" the others to work out meaning from context — that's how it knows "bank" means money or a river depending on the sentence.

It only predicts the next token

At heart, the model just guesses the most likely next token, again and again. There's no plan and no understanding like ours — it's very sophisticated autocomplete.

Mini glossary

Token
The small chunk of text the model works with — a word, part of a word, or a punctuation mark.
Token ID
The fixed number each token maps to in the model's vocabulary (often 50,000+ entries).
Embedding
The list of numbers a token becomes, positioning its meaning in the model's "map" of language.
Attention
The mechanism that lets each token weigh up every other token to capture context.
Parameters (weights)
The billions of numbers, tuned during training, that decide how signals flow through the network.
Inference
Running a trained model to get an answer — what happens every time you send a prompt.
