Four things to take away

One step, one job

Each step does a single, simple task well. Big results come from chaining small, reliable steps — not one giant prompt.

The output becomes the next input

A workflow passes the result of one step into the next. Order matters: summarise before you draft, not after.

Keep a human in the loop

Add a Human check before anything is sent or published. AI drafts; a person approves. That's where trust and safety live.

Reuse it

Once a workflow works, it saves time every single run. The minutes add up fast across a week of repeated tasks.

Mini glossary

Workflow
A set sequence of steps that turns an input into a finished result, the same way every time.
Step (or node)
One action in the workflow — summarise, draft, format — that does a single job.
Chaining
Feeding the output of one step in as the input of the next.
Human in the loop
A point where a person reviews and approves before the workflow continues.
Automation
Running a workflow with little or no manual effort, so it repeats reliably.
Trigger
The event that kicks a workflow off — a new email, a form, or you pressing Run.
