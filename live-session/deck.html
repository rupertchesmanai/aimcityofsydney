Part 1 · Foundations

The one formula behind every good prompt

Act · Explain · Please
  • Act as the useful expert — the role you want answering
  • Explain the context: the business, the situation, the constraints
  • Please do the specific task — and say what the output should look like
NewWeak result? Add a role, more context, the goal, the tone, the format. One of those five is almost always missing.
ConfidentSave your best prompts as reusable templates with blanks — your own swipe file beats writing from scratch every time.

Build A · Enquiry → response + booking

Turn a customer message into a booking

Act as the owner of my [café / salon / studio / trade]. Explain: here’s an enquiry from a customer asking about availability and pricing. Our tone is warm and helpful. Please draft a reply that answers what we can, asks the follow-up questions we need to lock in a booking, and suggests clear next steps.
[paste the enquiry]
ConfidentPaste a saved brand-voice block at the top so every reply sounds like you — then reuse it for every enquiry.
Everyone followsSafety

Build B · Feedback & reviews → insights

Turn reviews into things you can act on

Act as a customer-experience analyst. Explain: below are recent reviews and feedback for my business. Please group them into key themes — what people love and what frustrates them — and suggest three practical, low-cost improvements I could make this month.
[paste your reviews]
SafetyBefore you paste: strip customer names and personal details — keep the feedback, lose the private bits. And turn off “train on my data” in each tool’s settings.
Everyone followsConfident: save the template

Build C · Quote, invoice & send

From a client request to a quote, invoice & email

Act as my admin assistant. Explain: a client has asked for [describe the job]. My rates are [your rates]. Please create an itemised quote, a matching invoice with totals, and a short friendly email to send them. List any assumptions so I can check them.
AlwaysCheck the figures yourself before anything goes out — AI can get sums wrong. It drafts; you approve.
Everyone followsConfident: reuse weekly

Build D · Staff availability → roster

Build next week’s roster in minutes

Act as a duty manager. Explain: here are my staff, their availability, and our opening hours. Please build a simple weekly roster as a table, highlight any shifts I can’t cover, and draft a short message I can send each person with their shifts.
[paste availability + hours]
NewStart with one real week. Fix what it gets wrong, then keep the format — next week is a 2-minute job.

Part 3 · Cowork, set up for real

An AI that works across your files — with you watching

  • Point it at a folder on your computer — receipts, enquiries, documents
  • Give it a job in plain English: “sort these, draft a reply to each, summarise the lot”
  • It works step by step — you approve before anything is sent or saved
  • Start tiny: one folder, one repeatable task. Not your whole business at once.
NewYou don’t need this yet — just know it exists for when a task becomes a weekly grind.
